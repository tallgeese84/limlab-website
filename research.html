What we do

Four directions, one chromosome end

Structure · Cryo-EM

The telomere DNA replication machinery

Combining biochemistry, genetics, and cryo-EM, we run structure–function studies of human shelterin–telomere and CST–Polα-primase co-complexes to see how telomere repeats are made. We resolved one of the first structures of the human C-strand fill-in machinery — a preinitiation state showing how CST scaffolds Polα-primase on a telomere template.

He et al., Nature 608, 826–832 (2022)

Chromatin · Mapping

The telomere chromatin landscape

Telomere length is tied to many diseases, yet we still can't see how telomere-associated proteins distribute along these repetitive sequences. We're developing tools for high-resolution mapping of the telomere chromatin landscape and using them to probe how protein distribution governs length and end-protection.

NIH Director's New Innovator Award

Single-molecule · Biophysics

Watching shelterin assemble, one molecule at a time

Repetitive telomeric DNA presents thousands of near-identical binding sites — a problem for bulk biochemistry. Using AFM imaging and C-Trap (optical tweezers with confocal fluorescence), we watch human shelterin bind and move along long (>10 kbp) telomeric DNA, linking assembly dynamics to telomerase recruitment and T-loop formation.

Methods · Cryo-EM

Saving fragile samples at the air–water interface

Tardigrades and nematodes survive drying using LEA proteins. We repurpose them as additives during plunge-freezing to shield fragile, multi-subunit complexes from air–water interface damage — yielding cryo-EM maps that match or surpass current mitigation methods.

Abe et al., Nature Communications 15, 7705 (2024)
